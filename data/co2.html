CO2

CO2 is a very important gas. It is made out of one carbon atom and two oxygen atoms. It is a greenhouse gas, meaning that it contributes to the greenhouse effect. Plants use CO2 in photosynthesis. When they do this, it creates many byproducts, such as oxygen. This oxygen is then used by animals to breath. They in turn, breathe out CO2, and the cycle repeats. This is known as the Carbon Cycle.

Carbon dioxide can be found in

  • rocks,
  • animals,
  • plants,
  • water,
  • and the air.

Effects of Carbon Dioxide

When things like fossil fuels are burned for electricity, carbon dioxide is created as a byproduct. This can get into the atmosphere. When carbon dioxide gets into the atmosphere, it can trap heat. This is because it is a greenhouse gas. This causes global warming. Data from ice cores tells us that our atmosphere hasn't had this much carbon dioxide in at least 420000 years!
