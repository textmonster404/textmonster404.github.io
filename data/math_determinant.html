Determinant in Mathematics

In linear algebra, A determinant can be computed from the elements of a square matrix. The determinant of a matrix A is denoted by det(A) The determinant can be computed for a matrix using simple formulas. However, there is a geometric explanation for it.

When visualizing a 2x2 matrix on a plane as a transformation, imagine an object on the plane. The determinant of that transformation is the amount that the area of that object gets scaled after the transformation. If a transformation flips the plane, then we represent that as a negative number.

In 3 dimensions, it is almost the same, except we are dealing with volume instead of area.
